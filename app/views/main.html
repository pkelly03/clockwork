Nessa Smyth

Nessa qualified in the UK in 1999 with a first class honours degree in Physiotherapy and subsequently went on to achieve a Masters in Sports Physiotherapy and Manipulation from University College London in 2004. She has a wealth of experience working with athletes of all levels but also enjoys the challenge of treating and rehabilitating more chronic injuries that seemingly refuse to resolve.
  • London Olympics 2012 – Athletics
  • World Etchells Sailing – 2010
  • Chief Physio – Irish Mens Hockey (2002-2006)
  • Commonwealth Games – 2002
  • 1st Division Rugby (UK + IRL)

Injuries Treated

  • Sporting injuries – Treatment and Rehabilitation
  • Specialist in neck and back pain, including ‘whiplash’
  • Foot Biomechanic assessment(for plantarfasciitis, Achilles pain, shin splints etc.)
  • Shoulder injuries including ‘frozen shoulder’
  • ‘Tennis’ / ‘golfers’ elbow
  • Post-operative rehabilitation

Treatment Techniques

  • Spinal mobilisations and manipulations
  • Soft tissue mobilisations including Sports Massage
  • Acupuncture and Dry Needling
  • Orthotics
  • Taping & strapping including Kinesiology taping
  • Pilates
